2. Chemical vs Physical vs Hybrid: Complete Comparison

Chemical (Organic) Sunscreens

How they work: Absorb UV rays and convert them to heat

Pros:

  • No white cast
  • Lightweight, invisible finish
  • Better for daily wear and under makeup
  • Water-resistant formulas available

Cons:

  • Can irritate sensitive skin
  • Requires 20-minute wait time
  • May cause stinging around eyes

Best for: Normal, oily, combination skin

Physical (Mineral) Sunscreens

How they work: Create a physical barrier that reflects UV rays

Pros:

  • Gentle for sensitive skin
  • Works immediately upon application
  • Less likely to cause irritation
  • Reef-safe

Cons:

  • White cast (though modern formulas minimize this)
  • Can feel heavy or chalky
  • May pill under makeup

Best for: Sensitive, acne-prone, eczema-prone skin

Hybrid Sunscreens

Best of both worlds: Combine chemical and physical filters

Benefits: Better protection, reduced white cast, suitable for most skin types

5. Proper Application Technique

‚ö†Ô∏è 90% of people don't apply enough sunscreen!

The correct amount is 1/4 teaspoon (approximately 1.2ml) for face only. That's about two finger-lengths of product.

The 2-Finger Rule

  1. Squeeze sunscreen along your index and middle fingers
  2. That's the minimum amount for proper SPF protection
  3. Apply in dots all over face, then blend
  4. Don't forget: ears, neck, hands

‚úÖ Pro Tip: Apply sunscreen in two layers. First layer, let it dry for 1 minute, then apply second layer. This ensures full coverage.

7. FAQ: Your Questions Answered

Do I need sunscreen indoors?

Yes! UVA rays penetrate windows. If you're near a window or use electronic devices (blue light also ages skin), wear SPF.

How often should I reapply?

Every 2 hours if outdoors. Indoors, once in the morning is usually sufficient unless you're sweating or near windows all day.

Can I skip sunscreen if my foundation has SPF?

No. You'd need 7 layers of foundation to get the stated SPF. Always use dedicated sunscreen underneath.

What's the difference between SPF30 and SPF50?

SPF30 blocks 97% of UVB rays. SPF50 blocks 98%. The difference is small, but SPF50 provides longer protection time.

Do Korean sunscreens work for dark skin?

Yes! Most modern Korean sunscreens have no white cast. Look for chemical sunscreens like Beauty of Joseon Relief Sun.

Are Korean sunscreens reef-safe?

Chemical sunscreens typically aren't reef-safe. For ocean swimming, choose physical/mineral sunscreens with zinc oxide.
