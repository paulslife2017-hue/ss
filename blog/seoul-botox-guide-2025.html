Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: How much does Botox cost in Seoul?
A: Prices range from $80-$300 per area depending on the clinic and treatment area. Forehead: $120-200, Crow's feet: $100-180, Jawline: $200-400. This is 50-75% cheaper than Western countries.
Q2: Is Botox safe in Korea?
A: Yes! Korean clinics use only FDA-approved products (Allergan Botox, Nabota, Dysport). Korea has the world's strictest medical aesthetic regulations. Over 2 million procedures performed annually with 99.8% safety rate.
Q3: Do I need a Korean medical visa for Botox?
A: No! Botox is a non-surgical procedure. Tourist visa (K-ETA or visa-free entry) is sufficient. Medical visa (M-VISA) only required for surgeries or extended treatments (10+ days).
Q4: How long does Botox last?
A: Results typically last 3-4 months for facial areas (forehead, crow's feet) and 6-12 months for jawline slimming. Regular maintenance treatments every 3-4 months recommended.
Q5: Can I fly after Botox treatment?
A: Yes! You can fly immediately after Botox. However, avoid vigorous exercise, alcohol, and lying down flat for 4 hours post-treatment. Most patients return to normal activities within 24 hours.
Q6: What's the difference between Korean Botox brands?
A: Main brands: Allergan Botox (USA, gold standard, most expensive), Nabota (Korean, FDA-approved, 20% cheaper, same quality), Dysport (EU, spreads wider, good for large areas). All are safe and effective.
